The F-35 and the future of air combat

How new upgrades and increased production defy criticism and help the US maintain air dominance.

Posted by Ethan Biggs on April 17th, 2023

The F-35 Lightning II is already the most technologically advanced aircraft to ever fly, but the United States Air Force isn't just looking to shoot for the metaphorical moon, the stars are really their destination with the F-35 Joint Strike Fighter on deck to receive a series of upgrades that will truly push this aircraft into the next generation.

Lockheed Martin's ambitious stealth fighter has faced and continues to face criticism for it's bank-breaking budget and the widely touted notion that the program was a failure. Speak to any F-35 pilot or military strategist and you will hear a very different story. The capabilites the F-35 brings to the fight are invaluable to tomorrow's Air Force, regardless if the fighter ever sees combat.

Alex Hollings of Sandboxx News recently released a video discussing the implicaitons of upgrading an already industry leading set of systems on board the powerful fighter jet and what it means for the Air Force and military moving forward.
